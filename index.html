The Negative Path

The Falsification Principle

Richard Feynman observed that science is not the pursuit of being right, but the rigorous attempt to prove ourselves wrong. We can never achieve absolute proof of correctness; we only achieve the absence of disproof.

"We can only prove things wrong. We can't prove something is right."

Predicting Failure

The path to success is often veiled and infinite, but the path to failure is concrete. While you cannot simply declare what will make a person successful, you can identify with precision what will lead them to ruin. In a world of uncertainty, avoiding the wrong turns is our most reliable compass.

The Black Box

Reality is a black box. We build models to predict its output, mapping inputs (x) to results (y). When the results diverge, we face two possibilities: our path is fundamentally wrong, or our tools for observation are insufficient. We must constantly calibrate our technology and our perspective to strive toward the hidden truth.

Mastery vs. Effort

In life, input and output are often vaguely defined. Success in complex systems—like education—is not a function of hours spent, but of objectives mastered. True progress is found by identifying the core set of knowledge and solving the problems it presents, rather than simply measuring time in the struggle.
